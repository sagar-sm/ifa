A Message from the Director

October 31, 2012

You have weathered the storm and are now facing its aftermath. I know that many of you are coping with extremely difficult circumstances, with power outages and shortages of necessary resources. This is a stressful time and I greatly appreciate the courage and devotion of our entire community, particularly those staff members who have worked throughout these days, exchanging information and getting on with important tasks. We all owe an enormous debt of gratitude to the members of our building staff, Rob Doucette and Arturo Martinez, led by Wil Manzo, who stayed at the IFA throughout the storm looking after our buildings – separated from their families – to guarantee the safety of the Institute. Although the properties fared very well during the storm, they remain closed to all except essential staff until the University reopens on Monday, November 5. We are unable to provide proper security and so are not able to allow general access to the Institute. 

As you know from President Sexton’s letter, classes have been cancelled until Monday. Make-up sessions will be arranged. We deeply regret this disturbance to your studies, and know that this will cause some distress, but disruptions to transport and power supplies make it impossible to maintain a normal schedule.

For staff, the Institute follows University procedures. The University is closed through the weekend. With the subway and trains still out of commission and many of you stranded and coping with problems of all sorts, only essential personnel are required to report to work to protect our buildings and maintain public safety. You have already done great service for us and will be hearing from your supervisors about the transition from emergency operations to normal staffing routines and shifts during the course of the day. Administrators with internet access can respond to messages and carry on with tasks if possible – as many of you have been doing throughout the past few days.

Please keep safe.



Patricia Rubin
Judy & Michael Steinhardt Director
Institute of Fine Arts
